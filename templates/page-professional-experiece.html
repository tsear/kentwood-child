Write Act Repertory // Technical Director, 2021

Immediately following my Collegiate Graduation, as mentioned in my excerpt on my time at Mosaix, I moved to New York City. Initially, upon my arrival, I worked at Write Act Repertory as a part time Stage Hand earning some cash on the side. The work was very laborious & the hours were non-traditional, but the sense of control over logistical management that went into building a show into something presentable captivated me.

I worked under John Lant, proprietor of Write Act Repertory, an off-Broadway production house. Over the two & a hlaf years I worked with John, we worked on countless projects, doing some extremely cool things, as well as some extremely un-glamourous things. But I enjoyed every second of it -- Having gone through several show lifecycles as a stage hand, John offered me the role of Technical Director.

The role involved the technical management of the installation & tear-down of shows as they enter/exit venues in Hell's Kitchen, Manhattan. It may seem simple at surface level, but I assure you it is anything but.

Coordination starts in pre-production: Source equipment & skilled labor for lighting, audio, special effects, scene construction, set dressing, musical equipment, etc... totaling in hundreds of thousands of dollars in equipment, alone. All of which has to get loaded into trucks at their home locations & shipped into Manhattan, unloaded safely, & moved into the venue -- then you can start beginning the actual install. A typical install is around 48-72 hours end-to-end, from the time you load in, the opening night has a hard start date, so the TD, the Production Manager, Lighting Leads, Sound Teams, etc... all have to working in perfect conjunction to move their independent pieces of the puzzle into position to make something larger, something only possible through the combined efforts of all these extraneous pieces.

This role introduced me to logistical strategy & management in a way that truly revolutionized my outlook on professional life, & life as an individual.
